1. Scope – what Policies.bio actually covers

Policies.bio describes the **policy framework** for all sites operated by ronarn AS. That includes:

  • Educational .bio sites (e.g. Retinol.bio, Acne.bio, TestDNA.bio, Hyaluronic.bio, Niacinamide.bio).
  • Comparison & affiliate hubs (e.g. Retynol.com, Genectics.com, AcneHeals.com, Hylaronic.com, Niacinimide.com).
  • Supporting governance domains (e.g. PrivacyPolicy.bio, Terms.bio, Disclaimer.bio, Disclosure.bio, Transparency.bio).

Individual sites may have additional country- or product-specific policies where required by law.

2. Policy structure – how everything fits together

We separate legal, ethical and editorial responsibilities into different layers:

3. Which sites are covered by these policies?

Unless otherwise stated, the policy framework outlined here applies to all current and future ronarn AS properties, including but not limited to:

  • Skincare & dermatology education: Retinol.bio, Acne.bio, Hyaluronic.bio, Niacinamide.bio and related hubs.
  • Genetics & DNA testing: Genectics.com, TestDNA.bio, DNAKit.bio, DNAKit.com and related domains.
  • Health & lifestyle comparisons: AcneHeals.com, Hylaronic.com, Niacinimide.com and similar comparison projects.

New projects launched by ronarn AS will either inherit this policy stack or link to project-specific terms where necessary.

4. Policy updates, versioning & change logs

Laws, technology and business models evolve. When we update a policy, we aim to:

  • Update the relevant specialist site (for example PrivacyPolicy.bio or Terms.bio).
  • Record the “last updated” date near the top of that page.
  • Reflect major structural changes here on Policies.bio.

Where required by law, we may also provide in-product notices or email updates to registered users.

5. Complaints, corrections & escalation

If you believe a policy has been applied incorrectly, or you have concerns about compliance, you can:

  1. Start with the contact options listed on the relevant site (for example Genectics.com or Retynol.com).
  2. If the issue concerns network-wide policies, use ContactUs.bio.
  3. For serious governance or integrity concerns, you may refer to: Transparency.bio, Verification.bio and Audits.bio.

We aim to respond to reasonable, good-faith concerns as quickly as operationally possible.
